🏃‍♂️ Fitness & Races

I lift heavy things for fun and run long distances because I hate it. Being in motion isn’t just part of my life, it’s the part that keeps everything else moving. Endorphins > everything else.

  • Mumbai Half Marathon — 2025
  • Wipro Bengaluru Marathon — 2025

A few channels I would recommend (across categories) for fitness in general:

  • Jeff Nippard — science-based advice on workout splits, form, and hypertrophy
  • FITTR (Jitendra Chouksey) — no-fluff fitness advice (and coaching) tailored for the Indian audience
  • Saket Gokhale — pure motivation, with a side of learning
  • FitnessFAQs — all the calisthenics wisdom, zero small talk
  • KBoges — absolutely to-the-point guidance on form, exercises, and circuits
  • Yoga with Adriene — friendly, no-pressure yoga that somehow makes you want to keep coming back
  • Breathe with Sandy — guided breathwork for all levels

🎧 Podcasts I Keep Coming Back To

I like listening to learn and sometimes to fill the silence. These are the shows I keep looping back to—for curiosity, business, health and a laugh.

  • Huberman Lab — one-stop shop for health & performance
  • The Tim Ferriss Show — long-form conversations on craft, experiments, and life design
  • Stuff You Should Know — for the curious kid inside
  • The Grade Cricketer — two of my favourite topics—cricket and comedy
  • Acquired — cinematic deep-dives into how iconic businesses are built
  • Lenny’s Podcast — sharp chats on growth, product, and AI with operators who’ve shipped
  • Radiolab — big ideas in science, philosophy, and culture told like stories
  • This Past Weekend w/ Theo Von — occasional doses of Theo’s hilariously honest (and weird) takes

🎤 Stand-ups

Stand up is my second favourite art poison. Watched Russell Peters once and never looked back.

Here are a few I would recommend:

  • Jammin’ in New York (George Carlin) — GOAT material
  • You Are All Diseased (George Carlin) — considered cult by most
  • Jigsaw (Daniel Sloss) — a not-so-comfortable take on relationships
  • Fire in the Maternity Ward (Anthony Jeselnik) — a masterclass on misdirection
  • Elephant in the Room (Patrice O’Neal) — hilarious and fearless take on dating
  • Me Doing Standup (Norm Macdonald) — deadpan king
  • Let It Go (Bill Burr) — old Bill Burr at his best
  • Jealous of Sabziwala (Abhishek Upmanyu) — favourite Indian comedian
  • Kaksha Gyarvi (Zakir Khan) — heart, humor, and nostalgia
  • Sushi (Biswa Kalyan Rath) — genius deconstruction of everyday observations
